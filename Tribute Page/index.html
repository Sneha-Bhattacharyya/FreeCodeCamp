Mother Teresa

The Catholic nun who dedicated her life to alleviating poverty and caring for the world's most vulnerable.

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1910: Born Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu in Skopje, North Macedonia (then Ottoman Empire).
  • 1929: Arrives in India and begins her novitiate with the Loreto Sisters in Darjeeling.
  • 1931: Takes her first vows as Sister Teresa.
  • 1946: Experiences a "call within a call" to serve the poorest of the poor in Calcutta's slums.
  • 1948: Receives permission to leave the Loreto order and starts her own ministry.
  • 1950: Founds the Missionaries of Charity, dedicated to aiding the destitute.
  • 1957: Begins work specifically with lepers, founding homes for their care.
  • 1971: Awarded the Pope John XXIII Peace Prize.
  • 1979: Receives the Nobel Peace Prize for her work combating poverty and distress.
  • 1980: Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor.
  • 1982: Helps evacuate disabled children caught in the siege of Beirut.
  • 1985: Receives the Medal of Freedom, the highest U.S. civilian award.
  • 1997: Dies in Calcutta at the age of 87.
  • 2016: Canonized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta by Pope Francis.

"Mother Teresa wasn't a social worker. She was a religious fanatic, a fundamentalist Catholic who used her humanitarian work as a recruiting ground for the Church."

--- Christopher Hitchens, "The Missionary Position: Mother Teresa in Theory and Practice" (1995)
